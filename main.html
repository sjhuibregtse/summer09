Who is Ms. Sarah Huibregtse?

I've been a teacher since 2001, and a Computer Science teacher since starting at the Madison School District in 2005. With a Bachelor's degree from Lakeland College in Elementary Education I taught 4th grade for 3 great years at Cedar Grove-Belgium Elementary School. I then received my Masters in Computer Science Education from Cardinal Stritch University to extend my Computer Science certification through high school. I recently completed a certificate in Web Site Development from Madison Area Technical College and I am currently working on updating my Microsoft Office Certification to Office 2007.

While teaching at New Berlin I taught Computer Science Exploratory (HTML, Scratch, and Alice) to middle school students, as well as Introduction to Programming (Alice and Visual Basic), Web Design I (XTHML), II (XHTML and JavaScript), III (Beginning Dreamweaver, Flash, and Photoshop Elements), and IV (Advanced Dreamweaver, Flash, and Photoshop Elements), and AP Computer Science A (Alice and Java) to high school students. I was also the faculty advisor for New Berlin West's Modern Arts and Technology Club where we worked with the GameMaker program.

In addition, I teach Information Technology courses for Madison Area Technical College and have taught Microsoft Access 2007 and Careers in IT. I was also the administrator, faculty support, and trainer for New Berlin School District's Moodle site, which is an Online Course Management System. I am one of two teachers representing Wisconsin as a member of the Computer Science Teacher Association's Leadership Cohort. For two years I was also the Assistant Cross Country coach at Eisenhower Middle/High School.

Outside of school, I enjoy spending time with my family and friends. I enjoy running, biking (road & mountain), traveling, reading, computers, creating Web sites, digital photography (Flickr, WebShots), gardening, and playing with my cat (Tigger).

Prior to teaching I served in the United States Navy onboard the USS Mount Hood (AE-29), and in the Army National Guard with the 32nd Military Police Company. I also spent a few years traveling the country when I worked for a software company as a Training Specialist.

I am looking forward to learning and growing with all of my students (and parents) as the summer progresses.
